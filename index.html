The Impact of Generative AI on the Digital Transformation of Innovation Ecosystems in Morocco

A Doctoral Thesis Proposal

Overview

+

Problem Statement & Research Context

Generative Artificial Intelligence (Gen AI) is a transformative technology driving profound changes across sectors by enhancing efficiency, automating tasks, and fostering innovation. Morocco is actively pursuing a national digital transformation, notably through its "Maroc Digital 2030" strategy, which aims to establish the country as a digital hub by developing talent, digitalizing public services, and supporting tech SMEs.

Innovation ecosystems in Morocco—comprising enterprises, universities, startups, and government bodies—are central to this strategy. However, despite the recognized potential of Gen AI, a significant knowledge gap exists regarding its specific influence on the digital transformation of these ecosystems within the unique Moroccan context. Key sectors such as education, finance, and health face distinct challenges and opportunities in integrating Gen AI. This research will address this gap by identifying the conditions necessary for successful integration and exploring how Gen AI can be effectively leveraged within these collaborative innovation processes.

Purpose of the Study

The primary purpose of this doctoral thesis is to provide a deep understanding of the role of Gen AI in digital transformation within Morocco. It aims to achieve the following:

  • Identify best practices for effectively integrating AI into innovation processes across the education, finance, and health sectors.
  • Develop a theoretical framework to help organizations navigate their digital transformation efforts while fostering collaborative innovation.
  • Provide practical, actionable recommendations for stakeholders to maximize the benefits of Gen AI, considering Morocco's cultural and economic specificities.
  • Contribute new analytical frameworks to the academic literature on the interaction between Gen AI and innovation ecosystems.

Methodology and Roadmap

+

Research Question & Hypotheses

Main Research Question: How does Generative AI influence the digital transformation of innovation ecosystems in different sectors in Morocco?

To address this question, the research will test the following hypotheses:

  • Hypothesis 1: The integration of Gen AI enhances collaboration and knowledge sharing within Moroccan innovation ecosystems.
  • Hypothesis 2: Gen AI increases the adaptability and resilience of these ecosystems in response to technological changes and evolving user needs.
  • Hypothesis 3: The impact of Gen AI varies significantly depending on the sectoral characteristics and specific objectives of the education, finance, and health sectors.
  • Hypothesis 4: The adoption of Gen AI within Moroccan innovation ecosystems contributes to the emergence of new business models and innovative services.

Theoretical Framework

This research is grounded in three core theories to provide a multi-faceted analytical lens:

  • Innovation Ecosystem Theory: To understand how interactions between diverse actors (firms, institutions, users) create a collaborative environment and how Gen AI can amplify these synergies.
  • Complex Systems Theory: To view ecosystems as open, adaptive systems where Gen AI acts as a catalyst for adaptation and resilience in a complex, interconnected environment.
  • Contingency Theory: To argue that there is no one-size-fits-all approach. The effectiveness of Gen AI integration depends on specific contextual factors within each Moroccan sector (e.g., resources, skills, infrastructure).

Epistemological Stance & Methodology

The research adopts a hybrid epistemological stance combining post-positivism (knowledge is constructed through observation but is revisable) and interpretivism (understanding the subjective experiences of actors). This is suited to the rapidly evolving nature of AI.

A mixed-methods approach will be employed:

  • Qualitative Data: In-depth interviews with stakeholders and comparative case studies of innovation ecosystems in the education, health, and finance sectors in Morocco.
  • Quantitative Data: Analysis of relevant data to measure the impact of Gen AI adoption on key performance indicators (e.g., efficiency, innovation output).
  • Reasoning: A hypothetico-deductive approach will be used to test the formulated hypotheses against empirical data.

Research Roadmap

Year Key Activities
First Year Comprehensive literature review, precise definition of the research framework, and initial report for doctoral symposia. Potential submission of a state-of-the-art review article.
Second Year Presentation of a literature-based article, data collection (interviews, case analyses), and presentation of preliminary findings.
Third Year Presentation of an empirical article, in-depth data analysis, finalization of results, and thesis writing and defense.
